# Copy Writing

This document explains the copy and content for each section of the `public/index.html` landing page.

## Section 1 — Welcome / Hero

- `SYNC UP` label: reinforces the brand and product name.
- `welcome // optimization hub`: positions the experience as an onboarding and optimization destination.
- `mode: sync up.original` and `--:--:--`: UI details that create a dynamic, tech-focused atmosphere.
- Main headline: `sync up.`
- Subtext: `modern websites and google visibility for local businesses.`
  - This clarifies the core offering: website creation plus local Google visibility.
- Mode buttons:
  - `industrial` — targets logistics and industrial businesses.
  - `restaurants & food` — targets hospitality and restaurant owners.
  - `professional services` — targets lawyers, accountants, consultants, and other service firms.
- Ghost CTA note: `Click the buttons above to see different website looks` encourages interaction and explains the mode buttons.
- Primary CTA button: `[ Swipe Down ]` prompts users to continue through the page.

## Section 2 — Google Visibility Setup

- Top metadata labels: `google visibility setup`, `sync up core engine`, `south african local visibility`, `priority: high`.
  - These frame the section as the key visibility offering, with local relevance and urgency.
- Eyebrow label: `google visibility setup` restates the main service.
- Headline: `get your business found on google.`
  - Direct, benefit-focused value proposition.
- Description copy:
  - `Many businesses lose customers simply because people cannot find them online.`
  - `We create and optimize your Google Business profile so your business can appear on Google Search, Google Maps and local customer searches.`
  - Feature list: `business profile setup · maps visibility · WhatsApp and phone linking · category and business hours setup · profile optimization`.
  - This explains exactly what is included in the service.
- Price tag: `once-off setup · R500` states the cost clearly.
- CTAs:
  - `[ Setup My Business ]` encourages immediate conversion.
  - `see website features →` invites users to continue to the next section.

## Section 3 — Website Features

- Top labels: `infrastructure / features`, `sync up web engine`, `stack: fast performance · simple landing engines`, `preset: default`.
  - These labels position the section as the technical and design feature set.
- Headline: `professional websites designed around your business.`
  - This emphasizes tailored, business-centered site design.
- Supporting text: `We build fast, clean, mobile-friendly websites for Durban businesses. sync up builds simple landing layouts focused on professionalism, clear local visibility, and customer conversion.`
  - Reinforces speed, mobile-friendliness, local focus, and conversion.
- Feature cards:
  - `fast performance`: emphasizes mobile speed and customer experience.
  - `whatsapp integration`: promises easy contact through WhatsApp.
  - `seo foundations`: describes built-in structure for Google visibility.

## Section 4 — Operational Footprint / Process

- Top labels: `operational footprint`, `our process & payment`, `geo: durban & surrounding areas`, `ready to scale`.
  - Frames this section around geography, process clarity, and scalability.
- Eyebrow label: `businesses in south africa`.
- Headline: `Cape Town · Johannesburg · Durban · Pretoria · Bloemfontein · Polokwane · RSA`
  - Lists service regions to establish national reach.
- Dash grid cells:
  - `our process`
    - `pipeline`
    - Steps: 1) Understand your business, 2) Recommend the best setup, 3) Build your digital presence, 4) Launch and optimize properly.
    - This communicates a simple service workflow.
  - `payment structure`
    - `financials`
    - Details: `Visibility Setup: R500 once-off upfront`, `Website Projects: Upfront deposit → progress payment → final payment before deployment`.
    - This establishes budget transparency.
  - `initiate infrastructure`
    - `ready?`
    - Statement: `Whether you need better Google visibility or a professional website, sync up helps Durban businesses build a stronger online presence.`
    - CTA: `[ Contact sync up ]` opens the modal contact form.

## Section 5 — Contact Us

- Top labels: `contact us`, `get in touch`, `direct protocol`, `response within 24h`.
  - Sets expectation for contact and responsiveness.
- Form title: `Contact us`.
- Subtext: `Send us a message and we will get back to you shortly.`
  - Simple invitation to connect.
- Form fields:
  - Identity / First Name
  - Surname
  - Communication / Email
  - Contact Number
  - Executive Brief / Message
- Submit button: `Submit Protocol`.
  - Uses product tone aligned with the site’s technical branding.

## Section 6 — FAQ

- Top labels: `frequently asked questions`, `product clarity`, `billion dollar ui engineering`, `answers that scale`.
  - Conveys professionalism and product-level rigour.
- FAQ items:
  1. `what does sync up do?`
     - `Sync up delivers local business visibility and landing page systems built for speed, SEO, and easy customer contact.`
  2. `how fast can i launch?`
     - `Projects move quickly because the interface is engineered for modular deployment and focused conversion optimization.`
  3. `why is the ui called billion dollar?`
     - `We use institutional-grade interaction patterns, polished motion, and data-led layouts designed for enterprise confidence at scale.`
  4. `can you improve google maps visibility?`
     - `Yes. We set up the Google Business profile, map categories, hours, and contact channels to make your business easier to find locally.`

## Section 7 — About Us

- Top labels: `about us`, `who we are`, `design + engineering`, `Durban focus`.
  - Establishes brand purpose and location.
- Primary purpose block:
  - Label: `our purpose`
  - Headline: `We turn local business presence into memorable digital platforms with product-led confidence.`
  - Supporting copy: `Sync up exists to help Durban business owners feel equipped, visible, and ready to compete. We build editorial digital experiences that make discovery feel effortless and every visit feel like a confident step toward growth.`
  - Visual: logo image to reinforce brand identity.
- Vision / Impact block:
  - `Vision`: `To make local commerce feel polished, secure, and primed for discovery—without losing the warmth of the real-world business behind it.`
  - `Impact`: `We measure success by trust earned, clicks converted, and the clarity customers feel when they find your business online.`
- Story block:
  - Label: `our story`
  - Highlight: `Built from Durban, engineered for clarity.`
  - Body copy: `Our work is part systems thinking, part product craftsmanship. We blend design, performance, and local visibility so every landing page feels like a deliberate, confident presentation of what your business truly offers.`
  - Detail pills:
    - `Journey`: from quick site builds to thoughtful conversion systems.
    - `Mindset`: built around trust, transparency, and measurable growth.

## Page UI / Navigation Elements

- Sticky dot navigation: sections are labeled `welcome`, `visibility`, `websites`, `zones`, `faqs`, and `about us`.
  - Helps users move quickly through the page.
- `return-sticky` button: `↺ return to sync up original core`.
  - Provides a way to reset the experience or go back to the main theme.
- Cookie drawer: communicates consent options for essential session data and analytical tracking.

## Summary

This page uses a concise product narrative with a strong focus on local business visibility, website performance, and contact conversion. Each section supports a single idea: brand identity, visibility setup, website features, process clarity, contact, FAQ trust, and company story.
